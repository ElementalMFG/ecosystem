# Code of Conduct

The SS-SP community adopts the [Contributor Covenant v2.1](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/) with the following clarifications for our context.

## Our pledge

We commit to a harassment-free, respectful, technically-rigorous community. All contributors, regardless of background, ability, or level of experience, are welcome.

## Our standards

Positive behaviors we expect:

- Empathy and kindness.
- Respect for differing opinions, experience, and skill levels.
- Constructive feedback that improves the work.
- Accountability for mistakes; learn and move forward.
- Focus on what is best for the community and the users of our devices.

Unacceptable behaviors:

- Harassment (sexual, sexualized language or imagery, unwelcome advances).
- Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments, personal or political attacks.
- Public or private harassment.
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission.
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting.

## Clarifications for the SS-SP context

- **Standards work is neutral.** Official project channels (mailing lists, RFC discussions, chat, issues) are not appropriate venues for political demonstrations, campaigns, or unrelated activism. Personal opinions belong in personal spaces.
- **Safety over politeness.** We may bluntly say "this pin assignment is wrong" or "this crypto choice is insecure." Directness about correctness is not rudeness. Directness about people is.
- **Mesh safety.** Our devices go to first-responders, hikers, event organizers, and civilians in emergencies. Contributions that risk end-user safety (e.g. bypassing SOS priority, disabling anti-jamming) are subject to strict security review; carelessness here is a CoC issue.

## Enforcement

- Report to `conduct@ss-sp.org` (interim until the brand domain exists: `dylan@elementalmfg.com` — D-0012 in `governance/decisions.md`).
- The Community Council (3 members, distinct from the Steering Committee where possible) will:
  1. Acknowledge within 3 business days.
  2. Investigate confidentially.
  3. Apply proportional consequences per the Contributor Covenant enforcement ladder: private warning → public warning → temporary ban → permanent ban.
- Anonymous reports accepted via a third-party form (URL to be published).
- Appeals: to the Steering Committee.

## Enforcement roster

Governance Phase 0 (pre-community, per `06_GOVERNANCE.md` §2): the project maintainer acts as the interim Community Council of one, handling reports to `conduct@ss-sp.org` (interim: `dylan@elementalmfg.com` — D-0012) under the ladder above; incidents involving the maintainer personally are recused to an external, mutually-agreed mediator.

Phase 1 trigger: when the project has three or more regular maintainers, a three-member Community Council is seated (named in `governance/decisions.md`, distinct from the Steering Committee where possible) and this section is updated with the roster. Roster changes are recorded as governance decisions.

## Scope

This CoC applies to all official project spaces (repos, chats, mailing lists, conferences, meetups) and to conduct outside those spaces when it materially affects the community.
