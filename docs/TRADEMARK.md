# SS-SP Trademark Policy

*Draft — to be ratified at v1.0 and re-affirmed at Phase 2 foundation transition.*

## Marks covered

- **Word marks**: "SS-SP", "Seekie-Speakie", "Seekie", "Speakie", "SS-SP-Certified".
- **Design marks**: the shield/dot logo, the radar-cursor logo.
- **Colour palette** used with the marks.

Marks are owned by the founding SS-SP vendor entity, to be transferred to the neutral SS-SP Foundation at Phase 2 governance (see `06_GOVERNANCE.md`).

## Permitted uses (no permission required)

- **Nominative fair use** in documentation, tutorials, news articles, comparisons, and interoperability statements. Example: "This app works with SS-SP devices."
- **Unmodified redistribution** of official builds retaining the marks, provided nothing about the build has been altered.
- **Community-project self-identification** such as "for SS-SP" in third-party companion tools and plugins, so long as it does not imply endorsement or official status.

## Prohibited without written permission

- Naming a fork of the SS-SP project with the word marks (e.g. "SuperSS-SP", "SS-SP Enhanced").
- Using the design marks on hardware not manufactured under the official program or certified under the (future) SS-SP-Certified program.
- Selling merchandise displaying the marks.
- Registering domain names that use the marks in a way likely to cause confusion.
- Any use that implies partnership, endorsement, or official status not granted in writing.

## Third-party trademarks

- **"Meshtastic"** is a registered trademark of Meshtastic LLC. We use it only in nominative fair use ("SS-SP interoperates with Meshtastic v2.x networks"), never as a badge on hardware or in a way implying endorsement.
- **"Reticulum"**, **"LXMF"**, **"Nomad"**, **"Sideband"** are used only in factual references to those projects.
- **Silicon vendor marks** (Espressif, Morse Micro, Semtech, etc.) are the property of their respective owners and are used only descriptively.

## Enforcement

The SS-SP Foundation (or, pre-Phase-2, the founding vendor entity's Trademark Counsel) reserves the right to enforce these marks. Enforcement priorities:

1. Uses that endanger user safety (e.g. a counterfeit device using our marks).
2. Uses that confuse users into thinking a third-party build is official.
3. Uses that damage the reputation of the marks.

Enforcement will be proportionate and community-friendly. First response is a polite email; litigation is a last resort.
