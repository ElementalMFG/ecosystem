# SS-SP Contributor License Agreement

This is the Contributor License Agreement (CLA) for the SS-SP program,
granted to **Elemental MFG** (Idaho, USA — the parent entity per D-0012 in
[`governance/decisions.md`](../governance/decisions.md), "we"/"us"). It is
an Apache-style CLA: you keep your copyright; you give us the licenses the
project needs to ship, relicense per the published fork strategy
([`04_LICENSING_AND_FORK_STRATEGY.md`](../04_LICENSING_AND_FORK_STRATEGY.md)),
and later transfer stewardship to a foundation without re-asking every
contributor ([`06_GOVERNANCE.md`](../06_GOVERNANCE.md)).

**How you sign:** on your first pull request, the CLA bot posts a comment;
reply with the sentence it asks for and your signature is recorded (GitHub
username, timestamp) on the `cla-signatures` branch. Signing once covers all
your future contributions. This Markdown document is the normative text.

**CLA vs DCO:** both are required, and they do different jobs. The DCO
sign-off (`git commit -s`, checked per commit) certifies *provenance* — that
you are entitled to submit the change. This CLA (signed once, checked per PR)
grants *rights* — the license and patent grants below. There is no size
threshold: every pull request author signs the CLA.

## Corporate contributions

If you contribute in the course of your employment and your employer retains
rights in your work, your employer must authorize the contribution: an
authorized signer opens an issue titled `Corporate CLA: <company>` listing
the GitHub usernames covered, and agrees to the terms above on the entity's
behalf. We record the issue link alongside the individual signatures. A
standalone signable Corporate CLA document ships with the Phase 1 governance
transition.
